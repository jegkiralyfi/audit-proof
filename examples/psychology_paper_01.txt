Example Study on Sleep and Memory

Abstract
We studied whether sleep improves next-day recall. Participants completed a short memory task after either a sleep intervention or a control condition.

Methods
A total of N = 84 participants were randomly assigned to a sleep intervention group or a control group. The sample size was determined before data collection. Raw data and materials are available on OSF. We report Cohen's d and 95% CI for all primary outcomes.

Results
Participants in the intervention group showed improved recall, p = 0.03, Cohen's d = 0.42, 95% CI [0.10, 0.74].

Discussion
These findings support the hypothesis that sleep may improve next-day recall in this task.
